# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Neibaur Labs is committed to providing a respectful, constructive, and welcoming environment for everyone who participates in our projects and community spaces.

We expect all participants to act with care, professionalism, and good faith, whether they are opening issues, reviewing pull requests, discussing decisions, reporting security concerns, or otherwise representing the community.

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

- Treat others with respect and patience.
- Welcome different perspectives, backgrounds, and levels of experience.
- Give and receive constructive feedback thoughtfully.
- Focus criticism on ideas, code, documentation, and process rather than people.
- Take responsibility for mistakes, apologize when appropriate, and work to repair harm.
- Help keep discussions useful, inclusive, and aligned with the purpose of the project.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or sustained disruption.
- Personal attacks, derogatory comments, or demeaning language.
- Sexualized language or imagery, unwelcome sexual attention, or advances.
- Publishing private information without explicit permission.
- Bad-faith participation, including repeated inflammatory conduct or deliberate derailing of discussion.
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional community.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all Neibaur Labs project spaces, including repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, community channels, and any public space where someone is clearly representing Neibaur Labs or one of its projects.

## Reporting

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to:

hello@100dash.com

Reports will be reviewed promptly and handled as fairly and confidentially as possible. People responsible for enforcement must respect the privacy and safety of anyone who reports an incident.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may take appropriate action in response to behavior that violates this Code of Conduct. Depending on the circumstances, actions may include:

- A private clarification or correction.
- A formal warning.
- Temporary limits on participation.
- Removal of comments, issues, pull requests, or other contributions.
- Temporary or permanent exclusion from project spaces.

Enforcement decisions should be proportionate, consistent, and focused on protecting the health and safety of the community.
